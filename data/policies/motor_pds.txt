MOTOR VEHICLE INSURANCE — PRODUCT DISCLOSURE STATEMENT

SECTION 1 — ABOUT THIS POLICY
This policy provides insurance cover for your motor vehicle against loss, damage, and third-party liability. The insurer is AAI Limited ABN 48 005 297 807.

SECTION 2 — TYPES OF COVER

2.1 Comprehensive Cover
Comprehensive cover protects your vehicle against accidental loss or damage caused by:
- Collision, impact, or attempted theft
- Storm, hail, flood, lightning, and earthquake
- Fire and explosion
- Vandalism or malicious damage
- Animals striking your vehicle

Under comprehensive cover, storm damage to your vehicle is covered regardless of whether the vehicle was moving or stationary at the time of the event. Storm includes severe wind, rain, hail, and related weather events.

2.2 Third Party Fire and Theft
Covers damage caused to other people's property and vehicles. Also covers your vehicle if stolen or damaged by fire.

2.3 Third Party Property Damage
Covers damage you cause to other people's property or vehicles. Does not cover your own vehicle.

SECTION 3 — WHAT WE COVER

3.1 Storm and Weather Damage
Storm damage is covered under comprehensive policies when:
(a) The damage is directly caused by a recognised meteorological event
(b) The vehicle was in a lawful location at the time of the event
(c) The claim is lodged within 30 days of the event

A standard excess of $700 applies to all storm damage claims. A reduced excess of $350 applies when the vehicle was parked in a fully enclosed, locked garage at the time of the event — see clause 3.1(b).

3.2 Flood Events
Flood is defined as the covering of normally dry land by water that has escaped from the normal confines of any lake, river, creek, or other natural watercourse. Flood cover requires explicit selection at policy inception. If flood cover was not selected, flood damage is not covered.

Note: Rainwater runoff that causes localised water damage without overflow from a natural watercourse is classified as storm damage, not flood, and is covered under standard comprehensive policies.

3.3 Hail Damage
Hail damage to the vehicle body, glass, and windscreen is covered under comprehensive policies. Hail claims are subject to photographic evidence requirements.

SECTION 4 — EXCLUSIONS

4.1 General Exclusions
We do not cover loss or damage:
- Caused intentionally by you or anyone covered under this policy
- Arising from mechanical, structural, or electrical breakdown not caused by an insured event
- Caused by wear and tear, rust, or gradual deterioration
- Where the vehicle is used for ride-sharing services without prior endorsement
- Occurring while the vehicle is unlicensed, unregistered, or driven by an unlicensed driver

4.2 Fraud and Misrepresentation
If we determine a claim is fraudulent, exaggerated, or supported by false documentation, we will decline the claim in full and may cancel the policy. Prior claims history will be reported to industry data sharing registers.

SECTION 5 — EXCESS AND DEDUCTIBLES

5.1 Standard Excess
The standard excess for comprehensive motor insurance is $700. This applies to all claims unless a modified excess applies under the specific provisions of this policy.

5.2 Reduced Excess Conditions
A reduced excess of $350 applies when:
(a) The vehicle was garaged overnight (parked in an enclosed structure with lockable doors)
(b) An approved immobiliser is fitted and activated
(c) The claim does not involve a collision where you are at fault

5.3 Age-Based Excess
An additional excess applies for drivers under 25 years of age:
- Under 21: additional $600 excess
- 21–24: additional $300 excess

SECTION 6 — MAKING A CLAIM

6.1 Notification Requirements
You must notify us of any incident that may lead to a claim as soon as reasonably practicable, and in any case within:
- 30 days for theft and storm/weather events
- 7 days for traffic accidents involving third parties
- 24 hours for accidents involving injury to any person

6.2 Third-Party Liability
If another party is claiming against you following an accident, you must not admit liability, make any payment, or offer any settlement without our written consent. Failure to comply may result in rejection of the claim.

6.3 Evidence Requirements
Claims must be supported by:
- Photographs of the damage
- Police report (for theft, vandalism, or accidents involving injury)
- Repair quotes from approved repairers
- Your driver's licence and registration documents

SECTION 7 — VEHICLE REPLACEMENT

7.1 Total Loss
If your vehicle is assessed as a total loss, we will pay the agreed value or market value (whichever applies) minus the applicable excess and any outstanding premium.

7.2 Hire Car Benefit
Following a comprehensive claim, you are entitled to a hire car for up to 14 days while your vehicle is being repaired or assessed. The hire car benefit is capped at $80 per day.
